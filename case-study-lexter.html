The situation

Lexter is a legal talent intelligence platform. When I joined, we were testing a business strategy: could we build a tech-enabled recruitment practice as a way to dogfood our own SaaS platform? The idea was to use our internal database and tools to run recruitment — and let that real-world usage shape the product.

The problem was that legal recruiters have a terrible reputation. Lawyers have described them as a “necessary evil” and compared the experience to “having crypto bro energy.” We had no inbound leads, no brand credibility, and no connections in the lateral associate talent market. On top of that, we had a limited budget.

What we built

Working closely with the CEO (a seasoned legal recruiter), I built the entire outbound system from scratch — tech stack, automation, and messaging.

We used Lexter’s internal lawyer database to create segmented lists by seniority, location, and practice area. We wrote and tested personalized multi-channel sequences (email, LinkedIn, and phone) designed to sound human, not salesy. We automated lead notifications in Slack by combining manual scraping of 450+ job posting sites with Make.com and Airtable, so the team was instantly notified of opportunities at target firms.

The trust problem — and the content that solved it

Legal is a low-trust market. Lawyers don’t want to hear from recruiters. So instead of leading with a pitch, we led with something useful: a personalized Talent Snapshot Report for every search we ran.

Each report was tailored to a specific market, practice area, and bar call range — pulling real numbers from our database via Metabase. I assembled them manually in Canva using a template I’d built, but the data underneath was real and specific. It showed candidates what the talent landscape actually looked like in their market.

The reports served two purposes. First, they earned replies — even from lawyers who weren’t ready to move. People appreciated getting genuine market data instead of a cold pitch. Second, they kept the relationship warm. When we reached out again later, we weren’t strangers — we were the people who’d sent them something useful.

What the engagement looked like

Months 1–2 — System Design

Build the outbound infrastructure from scratch

Set up the tech stack: Airtable for pipeline tracking, Make.com for automation, folk CRM for contact management, OpenPhone for calls, and Slack for real-time alerts. Created segmented candidate lists from Lexter’s database. Built the first outreach sequences — email and LinkedIn — and tested messaging that sounded like a human, not a recruiter.

Months 2–4 — Content & Automation

Build the Talent Snapshot Reports and automate job scraping

Designed the report template in Canva and started pulling market data from Metabase. Automated Slack notifications for new job postings by scraping 450+ sources through Make.com. Integrated ChatGPT for call prep and email drafting. The reports became the anchor of every outreach sequence — the thing that turned cold emails into conversations.

Months 4–6 — Scale & Iteration

Run multi-channel sequences and refine what converts

Ran sequences across email, LinkedIn, and phone. Tested different angles, subject lines, and report formats. The 36% response rate showed that the trust-first approach was working — lawyers were replying even when they weren’t actively looking, because the content gave them a reason to engage.

Months 6–8 — Pipeline & Results

$215K in qualified pipeline. 14% conversion to interview.

The system generated $215K CAD in qualified pipeline within the test period. 14% of contacted lawyers were accepted by clients for interviews. The outbound engine was running — automated alerts feeding the team opportunities, AI-assisted prep making every conversation better, and content reports building the trust that cold outreach alone never could.

What got built

Multi-channel sequences

Personalized outreach across email, LinkedIn, and phone — designed to sound human, not salesy. Tested and iterated over 8 months to hit a 36% response rate.

Talent Snapshot Reports

Personalized market intelligence reports for every search — real data from Lexter’s database, assembled in Canva. The trust-building content that turned cold outreach into warm conversations.

Automation stack

Make.com scenarios for job scraping (450+ sources), Slack alerts, CRM syncing, and AI-assisted call prep. Turned a two-person team into a system that could operate at scale.

Segmented candidate lists

Built from Lexter’s internal database — segmented by seniority, location, and practice area. Every outreach was targeted, not spray-and-pray.

Pipeline tracking

Full-funnel visibility from first touch to client interview — tracked in Airtable with Metabase dashboards. Showed exactly where conversion happened and where it didn’t.

Slack alert system

Real-time notifications for high-value job postings scraped from 450+ sources. The team could act on opportunities within hours of them going live, not days.

The Takeaway

Recruitment is a relationship game. The outbound system got us in front of the right people, but the Talent Snapshot Reports are what kept the conversation going. When a candidate wasn’t interested in a role, we didn’t disappear — we followed up with something genuinely useful. That shift from “pitch and pray” to “lead with value” is what drove a 36% response rate in a market that actively screens out recruiter outreach.

The system also validated the tech-enabled recruitment model we were testing: two people with the right tools and the right content could generate $215K in qualified pipeline that a traditional team of five couldn’t match.
