The situation

Routific had built a route optimization platform that customers genuinely loved. PLG motion, SMB audience — people signed up for a free trial, and the product did most of the selling. They were approaching $900K ARR, almost entirely through organic search. Retention was solid. But the company's co-founder had been handling marketing solo — and she was about to go on maternity leave.

There was no marketing function to hand off. No dashboards, no KPIs, no documented ICP, no channel strategy beyond "organic search seems to be working." Routific was also a horizontal product — selling to anyone who needed route optimization, from florists to food delivery to field service. The positioning was broad because nobody had done the work to narrow it. And with a self-serve motion, every piece of the funnel mattered — from the first Google result to trial signup to activation. There was no sales team to paper over a leaky funnel.

I joined as the first marketing hire. The brief was essentially: figure out marketing. Build whatever needs to exist, sit at the leadership table, and make it work. Over four years the team grew to four — me, a product marketer, a content marketer, and a designer — but the function started from zero.

Before
  • No marketing KPIs or dashboards — no way to measure what was working
  • Horizontal positioning — selling to everyone, resonating with no one specifically
  • One channel (organic search) working by accident, not by strategy
  • Founder doing marketing part-time, about to go on leave
  • No experimentation practice — no structured way to test new channels or tactics
After
  • Marketing dashboards and KPIs in place — leadership had visibility into the funnel
  • Narrowed ICP to a focused segment that created clarity across the business
  • Organic search validated, scaled intentionally, and staffed with a dedicated content marketer
  • Full marketing function at the leadership table alongside product, engineering, sales, and CEO
  • Experimentation practice running structured tests across channels and trial activation

What the engagement looked like

Phase 1 — Foundation

Build the infrastructure that didn't exist

First priority: visibility. Built marketing dashboards and defined KPIs so leadership could actually see the funnel. Set up HubSpot properly. Created the measurement layer that every subsequent decision would depend on.

Phase 2 — Research & Positioning

Customer research that narrowed the target

Ran customer research to understand who was actually buying and why. Routific was horizontal — selling to florists, food delivery, field service, everyone. The research revealed a focused ICP segment that created clarity for the entire business, not just marketing. Refined positioning and messaging around that segment.

Phase 3 — Channel Validation

Test everything, then double down on what works

Built an experimentation practice — structured tests across channels and trial activation. With a PLG motion, trial optimization was as important as top-of-funnel. Ran multiple experiments over the years. The signal kept pointing back to organic search and trial. Rather than chasing the next shiny channel, validated the one that was already working and scaled it intentionally. Hired a content marketer to go deeper.

Phase 4 — Scale & Expand

Grow the engine and extend into new plays

Organic traffic and trial signups grew month-over-month. Managed lifecycle campaigns to improve activation and retention. Led the API relaunch through an ABM campaign targeting a new buyer segment. Captured COVID tailwinds when delivery logistics surged. By the end: $4M ARR, a real marketing function, and a system that didn't depend on one person improvising.

What got built

Positioning & messaging

Customer research translated into refined positioning, narrowed ICP, and messaging that worked across the website, trial experience, and sales conversations.

Marketing dashboards & KPIs

The measurement system that gave leadership visibility into the full funnel — from organic traffic through trial signup to activation and revenue.

Organic search engine

Validated organic as the primary channel, then scaled it — hiring a content marketer and growing trial signups month-over-month through SEO-driven content.

Experimentation practice

A structured approach to testing channels and optimizing trial activation — run experiments, read the signal, double down or move on.

Lifecycle campaigns

HubSpot-powered lifecycle emails targeting trial activation, onboarding, and retention. In a PLG motion with no sales team to catch drop-offs, this was the layer between acquisition and revenue.

API relaunch via ABM

Managed the go-to-market for Routific's API product through an account-based marketing campaign targeting a new buyer segment.

The Takeaway

Routific didn't need five channels. They needed one validated channel and a system around it. The instinct at early-stage is to spread thin — try everything, hope something sticks. What actually worked was the opposite: do the research, find the signal, build the infrastructure, and scale the thing that's already working. Four years, one primary channel, 4.4x ARR growth. Sometimes the boring answer is the right one.
